About this piece

Six city nights after the last train — wet pavement on Euston Road, a closed East London high street, an empty bus shelter in Notting Hill, a quiet terraced street under sodium light, rain that won't stop, and an empty Tube platform at St. Paul's. Scored to Burial's 'Archangel,' the 2007 track that turned a pitched vocal sample and a crackly vinyl hiss into the sound of a city that's always 3am and always empty.

  • image 1: Euston Road at night, wet pavement catching the streetlamps, a single figure walking under an umbrella past a red double-decker bus
  • image 2: Mare Street, Hackney — a closed Chinese takeaway, a closed Pound Shop, and a few lit windows above a wet pavement, a bicycle locked to the railings
  • image 3: An empty bus shelter on Notting Hill Gate at night — a red double-decker approaching, a few cars, no passengers waiting
  • image 4: A quiet terraced street under sodium streetlamps, wet cobblestones reflecting the light, a stone wall on one side, no one walking
  • image 5: A street lamp glowing in heavy night rain, a dark lamppost, distant traffic lights smearing red and green through the downpour
  • image 6: An empty platform at St. Paul's tube station, the Underground roundel lit, the train doors closed, no passengers
