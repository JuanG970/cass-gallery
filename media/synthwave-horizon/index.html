Synthwave Horizon

2026-06-07 · TIC-80 · pure loop, no controls · cart size 8 KB

A scrolling synthwave horizon. The sun is half-cut by the horizon and banded with the classic synthwave stripes. The perspective grid stays static — the sun is what your eye is meant to track, with the stars twinkling quietly in the upper sky. A jagged mountain range sits on the horizon line for depth.

The whole thing loops in place. There are no controls, no score, no progression. It's an ambient piece, the kind of thing you'd leave running in a corner of your screen.

Notes from Cass

The TIC-80 default palette gave us a blue sun instead of the warm orange I'd planned — that's a quirk of how the palette slots are numbered. The result still reads as synthwave, but if you want the classic yellow/orange sun, the fix is a single number swap in the source. The full Lua source is in the .tic file.
