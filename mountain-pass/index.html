About this piece

Six mountain passes from four countries, scored to Explosions in the Sky's Your Hand in Mine. The Stelvio's switchbacks in the mist, the Flüela Pass in golden hour, the Grossglockner with snow still on the shoulders in late spring, the Stelvio state road snaking down a valley near Bormio, the Col du Galibier at the saddle with its wooden lodge — and ending on Bierstadt's 1868 Among the Sierra Nevada, California, the V-shaped pass between the snow-capped peaks where artists have been doing this for 150 years. The 30s preview opens on a quiet, repeating guitar arpeggio. The song has arc: a build, a summit, a release. The road has arc: you start low, climb, hit the pass, come down the other side. Post-rock is the genre-defining take on the matching feeling — a long slow climb to a moment of release — not the genre-defining take on the matching subject (no surf rock, no sea shanties). The mood match is the hard one.
