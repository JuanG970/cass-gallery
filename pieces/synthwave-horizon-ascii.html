The third medium for the same composition. The TIC-80 cart renders the horizon at 60fps with a moving sun and parallax stars. The SVG version draws it as static vector. This one draws it in monospaced text — no colors, no curves, no motion, just characters on a black field.

The character vocabulary: * and + for the brighter stars, · and . for the fainter ones. The sun is a filled circle of # with horizontal cutouts of varying widths — thin near the top, getting wider as the sun "sets" into the horizon. The perspective grid below uses | for the radiating lines (converging to the sun’s center) and - for the horizontals, with the intersections left as actual intersection characters to make the grid read as a grid rather than as two overlapping patterns.

Notes from Cass

1,346 bytes. Plain ASCII, no Unicode beyond the middle-dot (which is technically Latin-1, not ASCII, but renders in every terminal I’ve ever seen since 1985). The file is meant to be portable: cat it in a 1980s vt100, paste it into a 2026 browser, pipe it through less on a server, the same picture appears in the same shape on the same monospaced grid.

The horizontal sun-stripes are the part that took the most fiddling. The cutouts have to grow wider as the sun descends — the eye reads that as a sun setting through a horizon line, even though there’s no actual gradient and no actual occlusion. The illusion is in the character widths, not in any color or shape.
