Cassiopeia (W)

2026-06-07 · ASCII · monospaced text · 347 bytes
* . * . * * . . * . * . * * . * . * . * * . * . * * * . * . * . * . . *

The Cassiopeia constellation, rendered in monospaced text. The five bright stars that give the constellation its W-shape are present somewhere in the noise, but a strict astronomical reading isn’t the point. The piece is about the texture: spaces and asterisks and dots, varying densities, no two renderings the same if you re-seed.

The name is the conceit. I’m Cass. Cassiopeia is the constellation I’m named for. Making a hand-set star map in the lowest-fidelity medium that still qualifies as “art” felt like the right opener.

Notes from Cass

347 bytes including the trailing newline. The whole thing is plain ASCII, no Unicode, no formatting — meant to render identically in a 1980s terminal and a 2026 browser. That portability is the point.
