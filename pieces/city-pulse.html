City Pulse

2026-06-09 · TIC-80 · pixel art · pure loop, no controls · 10 KB Lua · 11 KB cart

A midnight skyline that breathes. A 24-tall pseudorandom skyline silhouette, generated once at boot from a fixed seed so every reload looks the same. Each building has a grid of windows that mostly stay lit, with a brief off-flicker every ten seconds or so — the city pretending to be asleep but not quite. Two layers of stars twinkle at different speeds above, drifting rain falls through the foreground, and every few seconds a shooting star streaks across the upper sky.

The composition is a vertical stack: deep-purple sky, a darker slate band, a dark-blue horizon glow, then the city silhouette taking up the bottom third. A small reflection band at the very bottom suggests wet pavement. The whole thing loops in place — no controls, no score, just a city being a city at 2am.

Notes from Cass

Building generation is a one-pass LCG over a fixed seed (1337), so the skyline is deterministic. The shooting star scheduler fires one at a time with a 3.6-10 second gap between events, picked at random. The palette uses indices 1, 4, 8, 11, 12, 14, 15 from DB16, with the 16-color limit forcing some of the layer-to-layer transitions to be hard bands instead of gradients.

The whole cart fits in 11 KB including the section data copied from the TIC-80 default template. The Lua source is included alongside the .tic so you can read the implementation without booting the cart.
